Financial Glossary

  • Coast FIRE: A stage of financial independence where you have invested enough money early on that it will compound to fully support your retirement on its own. You can then "coast" by working a low-stress job that simply covers your current living expenses.
  • Roth IRA: An Individual Retirement Account funded with post-tax money. All growth inside the account, and all withdrawals made after age 59½, are 100% tax-free.
  • 401(k) / Roth 401(k): An employer-sponsored retirement plan. A Traditional 401(k) lowers your current tax burden, while a Roth 401(k) builds tax-free wealth for the future.
  • HSA (Health Savings Account): A highly potent investment vehicle paired with High Deductible Health Plans. Contributions are tax-deductible, growth is tax-free, and withdrawals for medical expenses are completely tax-free (triple-tax advantaged).
  • HYSA (High-Yield Savings Account): A liquid bank account that pays significantly higher interest rates than standard savings accounts, ideal for emergency funds and short-term milestone capital.

🏥 Healthcare Strategy: The HDHP & HSA Switch (Sept 2026 Open Enrollment)

In September 2026, you will have the opportunity to change your medical plan for the 2027 calendar year. Moving from your current plan to a High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) fundamentally alters your wealth-accumulation strategy.

1. What we discussed about the HSA for 2027

Currently, your workplace offers a Flexible Spending Account (FSA). FSAs are "use-it-or-lose-it" accounts that cannot be invested. By switching to an HDHP, you unlock access to a Health Savings Account (HSA).

The HSA is the ultimate triple-tax-advantaged vehicle. Contributions are pre-tax, growth is tax-free, and withdrawals for medical expenses are tax-free. Unlike an FSA, an HSA rolls over forever. In 2027, we will max this out (IRS Limit ~$4,300/year or ~$358/month) and automatically invest these pre-tax payroll dollars into broad-market index funds.

2. Cost Impact: Routine Checkups & Dental

Based on your provided insurance_info.pdf and Dental Coverage - Aetna.pdf documents, routine physicals and immunizations are covered at 100% with no copay and the deductible waived. Your Aetna dental plan covers 2 exams and 2 cleanings per year at 100%.

Under federal ACA law, HDHPs are legally required to provide this exact same 100% coverage for preventive care before the deductible. Therefore, since you only plan on attending routine checkups in 2027, your extra out-of-pocket cost for doctor and dentist visits will be exactly $0.

3. The Verdict: Should you make the switch?

YES. Absolutely.

Pros: You gain lower monthly paystub premiums (increasing cash flow) and unlock the greatest tax-advantaged investment account in existence (the HSA). Routine care remains entirely free.

Cons: If you get sick or injured and need non-preventive care (e.g., counseling, physical therapy, or a specialist), you lose the flat $30-$50 copays shown in your current plan. You will pay the full negotiated rate for those visits until your high deductible is met.
